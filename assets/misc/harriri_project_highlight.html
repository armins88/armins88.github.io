Project Spotlight

Writefree Founder and BU Alumnus, Chris Ackerman Talks About His Collaboration with the SPARK! Xperience Lab.

In the Summer of 2018, I was working in the Seaport at the Institute of Ethics with my friend and computer scientist, Armin Sabouri, when we had a lull in our day. When discussing potential projects, we discovered that we both wanted to create a web-based

note taking application, but with a twist. Within my personal and professional life, I knew so many talented individuals affected with dyslexia and had talked about building tools to help empower them. The solutions that exist to help people affected with dyslexia are either too costly, ineffective or for institutional use only. In our research, we have found that there are many independent tools and app settings that are convenient for people with dyslexia, but there is no single application built specifically for people with dyslexia. We set out to create the world’s first web-based note-taking application specifically designed to support people with dyslexia.

Without enough spare time to start this project on our own, I turned to BU SPARK!, an initiative that I have had the pleasure of knowing from very early on! The opportunity to provide guidance and real-world job experience to BU students was so exciting, as well as the ability to give back to Ziba Cranmer and everyone at SPARK! that I have had the opportunity to work with. As a project partner, I have met numerous talented and driven students. Without the resources and staff at SPARK!, I can confidently say that this project would only be an idea in our heads. Since the beginning of the project in the Fall of 2018, WriteFree has grown from an idea to an MVP with the help of SPARK! and a non-profit called NoticeAbility. With more than 100 beta

testers, we have begun to test the features and design that the students at SPARK! have helped us define and implement. Working with these passionate students and staff has been a wonderful experience, and we look forward to the numerous projects we will continue to propose with BU SPARK!
